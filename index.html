Who I am – the shorter version

I'm Mihai and I would describe myself as a tech and science enthusiast. What I do professionally is part of software engineering, in broad terms. My experience covers the roles of SysAdmin and DevOps. I'm mainly interested in the *NIX environment, the infrastructure built around it, cloud computing and information security. Another aspect to which I'm dedicated is the area of quantum information. I've been involved in research activities that led to multiple achievements, including publications.

I do have two degrees as well, but they don't define my background and skills.


Education and degrees

I'm a graduate of University POLITEHNICA of Bucharest. I received my BSc and MSc degrees from the Faculty of Automatic Control and Computers in 2018 and 2020, respectively. Aside from only a few notable exceptions, the academic experience is otherwise irrelevant. Still, I met several great people along the way, with whom I collaborated on various captivating projects.


Background and interests

I wrote both my Bachelor and Master theses on quantum information science topics, a field I've been passionate about for some years now. These have been by far the most stimulating parts for me as a student, primarily because they were personal choices and the field itself was not present within the curriculum, which increased my determination even more.

I've been working with GNU/Linux for more than five years. It started out as a genuine curiosity to dive into a novel world and it didn't take long to realize that it was a paradigm shift. There's no point in ever going back to anything non-Unix-like. It's about more than just an operating system, it's a way of life. On top of this, I'm inclined towards cryptography and networking.

Besides quantum theory, I also find relativity deserving of the "spotlight", given the mind-boggling implications and remarkable mathematical framework. Needless to say, they've already gained a (rather unfortunate) wide reputation in popular culture due to their public perception.


Who I am – the slightly longer version

I consider perseverance and wisdom to be among the highest values one should strive to embrace. I lead and advocate for a life free of useless distractions, guided by rational and moral principles and a sense of individual independence. To me, devotion to ideals is the path to self-realization. The comfort zone is easily appealing, but it doesn't mean much in the grand scheme of things, or "the bigger picture". There are experiences to be lived, people to meet, places to be seen and, naturally, lasting memories to be made. The world is too big to miss out on these.

Speaking of paths, many can lead to the same destination, but I believe the journey itself is what really shapes you. Unconventional paths require leaps of faith. What I pursue is the idea of delayed gratification, the prospect of having long-standing aspirations finally become reality. I'm confident going that extra mile eventually pays off.


Leisure

Watching movies and TV shows is a pleasant and common way to relax. Of course, I'm excited for highly anticipated feature films to get released and it's so good when they do live up to the hype. It's one reason we go to theaters, after all. On the somewhat opposite side of the spectrum, it's really satisfying to explore particular subgenres and discover "hidden gems". I rarely play video-games, though I occasionally check out walkthroughs of them. Outdoors, I enjoy cycling and just taking a relaxing walk in and around parks.

I read up on philosophy and history sometimes, when no other activity takes precedence. They are out there in the "background", but they will always be of interest to me, alongside other subjects. I'm especially intrigued by philosophy of mind and the history of the twentieth century.
